# Aike Logistics — 60-Second Recruitment Advert (Grok Video Production Pack)

A complete, scene-by-scene prompt pack for generating the Aike rider + fleet-partnership advert with Grok (or any AI video generator). Nigerian setting, standard English voiceover (no pidgin), 9:16 vertical, 60 seconds.

**The three real app screenshots are attached as pictures as part of this pack.** Whenever a phone screen appears in a scene, use the supplied screenshot as the device screen — never let the AI generate or redraw app UI. AI-invented interface text is the single fastest way for viewers to spot a fake.

## 1. Master prompt (paste as the global/style prompt)

> Cinematic vertical 9:16 advertisement, 60 seconds, shot like a real commercial on a gimbal-mounted full-frame camera, 60 fps, shallow depth of field, natural color grade with warm golden-hour tones. Location: Nassarawa GRA, Kano, Nigeria — serene  GRA ambience and very friendly streets, with black stripes in the background, keke napep tricycles, modern glass offices along the way. slight Traffic drives on the right; cars are left-hand drive. All people are Nigerian with Nigerian accent not ghanian accent, with natural skin texture, realistic hands with five fingers, and authentic body language — no uncanny smiles, no plastic skin.
>
> RECURRING CHARACTERS (keep faces,clothings,devices, builds and wardrobe identical in every scene):
> — IBRAHIM, the rider: Nigerian man, late twenties, medium build, short hair,short trimmed beards, warm confident face. Wardrobe: navy-blue rider jacket over a white t-shirt, dark trousers, black open-face helmet (visor up when talking), black gloves tucked in pocket. Rides a clean red-and-black 150cc delivery motorcycle with a black delivery box branded with Aike.

> — MRS. ADEBAYO, the operations manager: Nigerian woman, around forty, natural hair pulled back, smart blouse and ID lanyard, tablet in hand. Appears only in the fleet segment.
> — FUNKE, the customer: Nigerian woman, thirties, casual smart dress. Appears only in the handover scene.
>
> IBRAHIM's phone is one consistent device throughout: a modern dark-blue Android smartphone of below characteristics Height: 160 mm Width: 75 mm Thickness: 8 mm Screen size: approximately 6.5 inches diagonally Aspect ratio: 20:9 Corner radius: approximately 8–10 mm, the phone is in a plain black case. Whenever its screen is visible, the screen content is the supplied app screenshot for that scene — sharp, unmodified, correctly perspective-mapped to the device. Do not invent, redraw, translate or restyle any interface text.
>
> Audio: upbeat afrobeats-inflected instrumental (percussion, log drum, warm keys), real Kano street ambience under it (engines, distant horns, market murmur), and a clear, warm Nigerian female voiceover in standard English (Nigerian accent not ghanian accent) — professional advert read, not pidgin. Every voiceover line is also burned in as clean subtitles.
>
> Background signage must be either soft-blurred or limited to short real English words ("BARBER", "PHARMACY", "SUYA"). Vehicle plates follow the Lagos format but are always partially out of focus. No brand logos other than Aike.

## 2. Scene-by-scene breakdown (0:00 – 1:00)

### Scene 1 — The city moves (0:00 – 0:04)
- **Location/action:** Dawn over a mainland Lagos street. Time-lapse-feel opening:  shop shutters rolling up, a keke crossing frame. Cut to IBRAHIM outside his home in a typical nigerian street, strapping on his helmet, tapping the delivery box twice like a ritual.
- **Camera:** Wide establishing drone-style push-in, then cut to a tight low-angle on the helmet clasp clicking shut.
- **Voice Over:** "Every day, Kano moves. And somebody has to move it."
- **On-screen text:** none (let the city breathe).
- **Sound:** Music starts on the helmet click. Street ambience.

### Scene 2 — Meet Ibrahim (0:04 – 0:09)
- **Location/action:** IBRAHIM kicks the bike to life and pulls into morning traffic, riding confidently but safely, weaving past a parked danfo. He glances at the camera with a small nod — friendly, not showy.
- **Camera:** Tracking shot alongside the moving bike at handlebar height, then rising slightly.
- **Voice Over:** "Meet Ibrahim. He rides with Aike — the delivery platform that puts riders first."
- **On-screen text:** **RIDE WITH AIKE** (bottom third, 3 words, bold, brand sky-blue #0ea5e9 on white pill).
- **Sound:** Engine note under music.

### Scene 3 — Signing up (0:09 – 0:14) — SCREEN INSERT `01-registration.png`
- **Location/action:** Flashback framing: IBRAHIM at a small table at home, phone in hand, thumb scrolling the Aike sign-up form. Close-up over his shoulder: the phone screen shows **the supplied registration screenshot** (Rider option highlighted in blue, "Sign up with Google" button visible). He taps once, satisfied.
- **Camera:** Over-shoulder macro on the phone, rack focus from his face to the screen. The screen must fill most of the frame for at least 1.5 seconds.
- **Voice Over:** "Signing up is simple. Register, verify your identity, and you are ready to earn."
- **On-screen text:** **SIGN UP → VERIFY → EARN**
- **Sound:** Soft UI tap sound.

### Scene 4 — The offer (0:14 – 0:20) — SCREEN INSERT `03-accept-payment.png`
- **Location/action:** Present day. IBRAHIM parked at the roadside; his phone buzzes in its handlebar mount. Insert shot: **the supplied offer screenshot** — fee ₦3,825.00 at the top, pickup "12 Allen Avenue, Ikeja", delivery "5 Admiralty Way, Lekki Phase 1", green ACCEPT OFFER button. His gloved thumb taps ACCEPT OFFER. He checks his mirror and pulls out.
- **Camera:** Phone-mount close-up (screen readable, straight-on, no glare), then whip to the bike accelerating away.
- **Voice Over:** "Delivery requests come straight to his phone — with the fee shown upfront. He taps accept, and he is moving."
- **On-screen text:** **SEE THE FEE BEFORE YOU ACCEPT ✓**
- **Sound:** Notification chime (two-tone), engine pull-away.

### Scene 5 — Deliver well, rise higher (0:20 – 0:26)
- **Location/action:** Quick montage: bike crossing a bridge with the lagoon behind; IBRAHIM handing a neat package to FUNKE at her gate — she smiles, he gives a small salute; her finger taps a fifth star on her phone (generic star row only — five gold stars, no other UI text).
- **Camera:** Three fast cuts: wide bridge tracking shot → waist-level handover two-shot → macro of the star tap.
- **Voice Over:** "Every delivery builds his rating. And every rating brings the next job to him."
- **On-screen text:** **★★★★★ GOOD WORK WINS MORE WORK**
- **Sound:** Music lifts; soft star "ding".

### Scene 6 — Payment confirmed (0:26 – 0:32) — SCREEN INSERT `03-accept-payment.png`
- **Location/action:** IBRAHIM parked in shade, helmet visor up, drinks water, checks his phone. Insert: **the supplied confirm-payment screenshot** — "Payment received. Confirm below to close out this job." with the green Confirm Payment Received button. His thumb presses it. A satisfied exhale.
- **Camera:** Mid shot establishing the pause, then clean straight-on phone insert filling the frame.
- **Voice Over:** "When the job is done and payment lands, he confirms it — and the money enters his wallet."
- **On-screen text:** **PAYMENT CONFIRMED ✓**
- **Sound:** Positive confirmation tone.

### Scene 7 — Withdraw daily (0:32 – 0:38) — SCREEN INSERT `04-withdrawal-success.png`
- **Location/action:** Same position, one beat later. Insert: **the supplied wallet screenshot** — green banner "Withdrawal request submitted…", Available Balance ₦10,825.00, Total Earned ₦17,325.00. Cut back to IBRAHIM's face: a small, real smile as he pockets the phone.

- **Voice Over:** "And his money? He withdraws it daily — straight to his bank account."
- **On-screen text:** **WITHDRAW DAILY 🏦**

### Scene 8 — The fleet pivot (0:38 – 0:46)
- **Location/action:** Hard cut to a modern logistics yard/office forecourt: MRS. ADEBAYO walks past a line of five parked delivery motorcycles with riders in matching hi-vis vests doing morning checks. She reviews her tablet (screen angled away — no UI visible), nods to the riders; they mount up and stream out of the gate in sequence.
- **Camera:** Steadicam walking shot with her, then a wide of the riders rolling out — the "power shot" of the advert.
- **Voice Over:** "Run a logistics company? Put your whole fleet on Aike. Live tracking, per-rider ratings, and daily settlement — one platform for every rider you manage."
- **On-screen text:** **FLEET OWNER? PARTNER WITH AIKE** (distinct darker banner styling).

### Scene 9 — Two audiences, one platform (0:46 – 0:53)
- **Location/action:** Split-screen: left, IBRAHIM riding through golden-hour traffic; right, MRS. ADEBAYO's riders fanning out across an interchange. The two halves merge into a single wide city shot as the sun flares.
- **Camera:** Matched-speed tracking shots for a clean split-screen; merge on a rising drone-style wide.
- **Voice Over:** "Whether you ride for yourself, or you manage a fleet — Aike keeps Nigeria moving."
- **On-screen text:** **RIDERS • FLEETS • ONE PLATFORM**

### Scene 10 — End card (0:53 – 1:00)

- **Camera:** Static, 7 full seconds. Nothing moves except a subtle light sweep.
- **Voice Over:** "Sign up today at entrepoints dot N-G, slash aike, slash register. Aike Logistics. Your ride. Your time. Your money."
- **On-screen text (added in edit):** **entrepoints.ng/aike/register** (large), beneath it **Fleet owner? Partner with us.**, QR code bottom-right.
- **Sound:** Final beat lands at 0:55; logo sting; two seconds of clean hold.

## 3. Anti-AI-artifact directives (append to every generation)

> Realism requirements — reject and regenerate any shot that violates these:
> - Hands always have five fingers; grips on handlebars and phones must be anatomically correct.
> - The same actor faces, hairstyles and wardrobe in every scene they appear in; no drifting facial features between cuts.
> - No generated text anywhere: phone screens use only the supplied screenshots; background signage is blurred or limited to single real English words; the end-card URL is added in post, not generated.
> - Motorcycle wheels, spokes and mirrors stay geometrically stable in motion; no morphing frames.
> - Traffic keeps right; steering wheels on the left; number plates soft-focused.
> - Natural Nigerian skin tones with realistic texture — a light sweat sheen is good; waxy or over-smoothed skin is not.
> - Shadows anchored to their objects; consistent sun direction (morning scenes 1–4, midday 5–7, golden hour 8–9).
> - No extra limbs, no floating helmets, no duplicated background pedestrians walking in sync.
> - Phone is the same dark-blue device in a black case every time it appears.

## 4. Production notes (for whoever runs Grok and the edit)

1. **Generate scene-by-scene**, not in one pass — AI video holds quality for short clips. Reuse the master prompt plus the single scene block each time; keep the same seed/character reference where the tool supports it.
2. **Screen inserts:** if the generator cannot map a supplied image onto the phone screen convincingly, generate the shot with a plain dark phone screen and composite the screenshot in the edit (simple corner-pin track in CapCut/Premiere). The screenshots are 1170×2532 — crop to the visible screen area of each scene's framing.
3. **Voiceover:** record the ten VO lines as one continuous read by a Nigerian voice artist (or a high-quality TTS voice), then cut to picture. Total read is ~140 words ≈ 55 seconds at advert pace.
4. **Subtitles:** burn in for the full film — most vertical video plays muted.
5. **Music:** licensed afrobeats-style instrumental; duck under VO by 6–8 dB.
6. **End card:** hold a full 7 seconds; URL `entrepoints.ng/aike/register` in the heaviest weight after the logo; QR code links to the same URL.
7. **Cutdown:** for a 30-second version, keep scenes 1, 4, 7, 8 and 10 and trim VO lines to match.

## 5. Messaging guardrails (do not break in any re-edit)

- Never mention any percentage split of the delivery fee.
- "Withdraw daily — straight to your bank account" is the payout claim; never say "instant withdrawal".
- Never quote per-kilometre or minimum-fare naira rates as marketing copy (fees visible inside the real screenshots are fine — they are the product, not a claim).
- Fleet segment is a call to partner/contact, not a promise of fleet dashboards or bulk tools.
- Standard English throughout; no pidgin.
